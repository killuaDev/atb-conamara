The Living Language - Conamara

3. Pronunciation of Consonants

3.1 Broad bh

Broad bh = /v/ usually: ábhar, bhuail, taobh. There's very little friction except before consonants (bhraith) or at the end of a word (sliabh); outside of those two places it's almost identical to the English /w/ in woe.

/u:/ is common in place of an epenthetic vowel + /v/: banbh (banú nó banabh), garbh, marbh, tarbh. leanbh is said as leana.

-bha- = /u:/: arbhar (arúr), colbha, gealbhan, mearbhall

3.2 Slender bh

Slender bh = /v´/: bhí, sléibhe (genitive), bainbh (bainibh) (plural). There's a silent bh in scríbhneoir. /b´/ usually in libh, sibh etc.

3.3 Broad ch

Broad ch = /x/ in every position: achar, bealach, chas, locht. /h/ in chuig, chugam, etc.

3.4 Slender ch

Slender ch = /x´/ at the start of a word: cheap, chreid. But /h/ in cheana.

/h/ in the middle of a word usually: dícheall, droichead (3.20). But /x´/ sometimes in fiche, oíche. /f´/ in cluiche, doicheall. The ch is silent in deich.

3.5 Broad dh

Broad dh = /y/ at the start of a word: dhúin.

dh is silent at the end of a noun: *briseadh (brise), casadh (casa), samhradh (samhra).

Regarding broad -dh in verbs see 13.2.

3.6 Slender dh

Slender dh = /j/ at the start of a word: dhíol. There's no friction except before a consonant: mo dhream.

Slender dh is silent at the end of a word: anuraidh (anura), imeoidh (imeo), réidh (ré), ó thuaidh (ó thua).

3.7 Broad f

Broad f = /f/ usually: fuar, lofa. Silent or = /h/ in fiafraí (fiarthaí)

-fa- = /u:/ in méanfach (méanúch).

3.8 Slender f

Slender f = /f´/: fíor, deifir, geaif. /h/ in féin normally, but /p´/ sometimes following *m: liom féin (liom péin), táim féin (táim péin).

Regarding -f- in verbs see 13.4-6.

Broad gh

Broad gh = /y/ at the start of a word: ghoid. Regarding gh in the middle of a word see 2.9, 2.11.

-gha- = /i:/ carghas (caraíos).

Slender gh

Slender gh = /j/ at the start of a word: ghearr. There's little friction except before a consonant: ghread.

Slender gh is silent at the end of a word: bealaigh (beala) (genitive), imigh (ime), uaigh (ua).

/h/ is used at the end of amuigh, istigh before vowel: amuigh ann (amuth ann). Sometimes those two words have /x´/ (especially in Carna): amuich, istich are said.

3.11 Broad l

Broad l = dental /l/ in all positions normally: balla, geall, lá, slán. But alveolar /l/ sometimes in Carna in words like glan, seol, talamh. Usually /h/ follows the alveolar /l/ in Carna and in other places in words like boladh, folamh, talamh.

3.12 Slender l

Slender l = Palatal /L´/ in the following positions usually: at the start of a word (leas), following s (sliabh), before t (ceilt), where ll is in the spelling (buille). Alveolar /l´/ in other positions: buile, cáil, cleas. /l´/ in le (liom, etc), tuilleadh (tile), and in loans like lad.

3.13 Broad mh

Broad mh = /v/: mhair, fómhar, lámh. There is little friction except before a consonant: samhradh /savrə/ (2.10).

Silent mh in clúmhach (clúch), cumha (cú), cumhachta (cúchta). Also in slachtmhar (slachtar).

-mha- = /u:/ in greannmhar (greannúr).

Silent mh at the end of two syllable words normally: annamh (anna), gaineamh (gaine), talamh (tala).

3.14 Slender mh

Slender mh = /v´/: mheas, deimhin, láimh.

Silent mh in Gaillimh (Gaille) but it's preserved in the genitive (Gaillimhe). The mh is dropped in Muimhneach (Muíneach), and sometimes in cuimhne (cuíne).

3.15 Broad n

Broad n = dental /n/ in every position normally: annamh, gann, nós, snámh. But alveolar /n/ someitimes (especially in Carna) in words like anam, bán, scanradh, scian. Also in loans like nobby, nurse.

n = /r/ usually in words like cnoc, gnaíúil, mná; but /n/ in gnás, gnáth.

3.16 Slender n

Slender n = Palatal /N´/ in the following positions: at the start of a word (neart), following s (sneachta), before t, (caint), where nn is in the spelling (rinne).

Alveolar /n´/ in other positions: cáin, duine, léine. /n´/ also in anois.

n = /r´/ usually in words like cneasta, cniteáil, imní.

3.17 ng

Broad ng = /ŋ/ at the start of a word (i ngarraí), /ŋg/ usually in the middle of a word (ceangal) and at the end of a word (rang).

The same way with slender ng = /ŋ´/ at the start of a word (i ngleann), /ŋg´/ in the middle of a word (daingean) and at the end of a word (moing). pingin is said as pínn.

ng = /N´/ in fairsing, tarraing.

3.18 r

r = Broad /r/ at the start of a word: reo, rí, rith. Slender /r´/ in ariamh, aríst; but broad /r/ in the first r of aréir. r is always broad before d, l, n, s, t: aird, airneán, beirt, comhairle, tuirse. Broad r also in beirthe, faraoir (faríor), uirthi (orha).

3.19 s

/ʃ/ is in the group sr- in some places (Carna): sráid, srian. But /s/ in that group in other places. Always /s/ in sm-, sp-: smig, speal. /ʃ/ normally in sc- (scéal, scil), but /s/ sometimes in Cois Fharraige.

3.20 th

th = /h/ at the start of a word: theann /ha:n/, theastaigh, thit. /x´/ before /u:/ in thiúrfadh (14.10).

/h/ in the middle of a word too usually: athair, bóthar, láthair. But in Cois Fharraige the /h/ is dropped often. Sometimes the vowel is changed become of that: cathaoir /kair´/, droichead /dre:d/.

In words like aithne, athrú, the /h/ comes after the n, r: aithne (ainthe). The /h/ is usually dropped in Cois Fharraige.

The /h/ normally isn't audible at the end of a word: (tá go) maith. But it is usually audible if a vowel follows it: is maith é.

  • th = /x´/ sometimes in ar bith, d'ith, rith (Carna).
  • th = /f/ in sraothartach, sometimes in leathuair.
  • th = /f´/ in tuathal (tuaifeal).
